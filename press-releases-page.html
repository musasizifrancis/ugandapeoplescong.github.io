UPC Calls for National Dialogue on Electoral Reforms

Official Statement Electoral Reform
April 15, 2025

The Uganda People's Congress (UPC) is calling for a comprehensive national dialogue on electoral reforms ahead of the 2026 general elections. In a statement issued by Party President Jimmy Akena, UPC emphasized the need for inclusive discussions involving all political stakeholders to ensure a free and fair electoral process.

"For democracy to thrive in Uganda, we must address the persistent challenges in our electoral system. UPC proposes a series of reforms including the restructuring of the Electoral Commission, implementation of electronic voting systems, and a review of campaign financing regulations," said Akena.

UPC Condemns Rising Cost of Living and Government Inaction

Economy Social Welfare
April 3, 2025

The Uganda People's Congress has expressed deep concern over the escalating cost of living affecting ordinary Ugandans. In a statement released today, UPC Secretary General Fred Ebil highlighted the party's dissatisfaction with the government's handling of economic challenges facing the country.

"The continuously rising prices of essential commodities are pushing more Ugandans into poverty. The current administration has failed to implement effective economic policies to address these challenges. UPC proposes immediate interventions including reduction in fuel taxes, agricultural subsidies, and price controls on essential commodities," stated the release.

UPC Announces Nationwide Party Membership Drive

Party Affairs Mobilization
March 22, 2025

The Uganda People's Congress has launched a nationwide membership drive aimed at strengthening the party's grassroots presence across the country. Speaking at the launch event at the party headquarters in Kampala, UPC President Jimmy Akena announced an ambitious plan to register one million new members by the end of 2025.

"Today marks the beginning of a new chapter in UPC's journey. We are reaching out to Ugandans from all walks of life who believe in our vision of a united, prosperous, and democratic Uganda. Our membership drive will focus on youth engagement, women empowerment, and digital registration to make the process accessible to all," Akena stated.

UPC Commemorates 65th Anniversary of Its Founding

Party History Celebration
March 9, 2025

The Uganda People's Congress celebrated the 65th anniversary of its founding with events held across the country. The party, which played a pivotal role in Uganda's independence struggle and subsequent political development, organized a series of commemorative activities reflecting on its rich history and charting the way forward.

In his anniversary message, UPC President Jimmy Akena paid tribute to the party's founder, Dr. Apollo Milton Obote, and other founding members who laid the foundation for one of Uganda's oldest political parties. "As we celebrate 65 years of existence, we recommit ourselves to the ideals of national unity, social justice, and economic progress that have guided our party since its inception," Akena said.
