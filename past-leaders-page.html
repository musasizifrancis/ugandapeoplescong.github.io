PAST LEADERS OF UPC

The visionaries who shaped our party's history

Legacy of Leadership

Since its founding in 1960, the Uganda People's Congress has been led by distinguished individuals who have shaped not only the party but also the nation's history. These leaders have steered the party through Uganda's independence, political transitions, challenges, and resurgence.

Dr. Apollo Milton Obote

Founding President

1960 - 1985

Dr. Apollo Milton Obote was the founding president of the Uganda People's Congress and the first Prime Minister of independent Uganda. He led the party through independence and two terms as the country's president, with his leadership defining much of Uganda's early post-colonial history.

Learn More

Cecilia Ogwal

Party President (Interim)

1985 - 1992

Cecilia Ogwal served as the interim president of UPC following Dr. Obote's exile. Known as the "Iron Lady," she maintained the party's relevance during a challenging period in Uganda's political landscape, advocating for democratic principles and multi-party politics.

Learn More

Dr. Olara Otunnu

Party President

2010 - 2015

Dr. Olara Otunnu, a former UN Under-Secretary-General, led UPC with a vision for national reconciliation and democratic reform. His international experience brought a global perspective to the party's policies and approach to governance.

Learn More

Miria Obote

Party President

2005 - 2010

Miria Obote, widow of Milton Obote, led the party with resilience and determination to maintain its historical legacy. During her leadership, she worked to unite different factions within the party and position UPC as a significant opposition force.

Learn More

Dr. James Rwanyarare

Acting Chairman

1992 - 2005

Dr. James Rwanyarare played a crucial role in maintaining the UPC's presence during a period when political parties faced significant restrictions. His steadfast commitment to multi-party democracy helped keep the party's ideals alive.

Learn More

Joseph Bbosa

Vice President

2010 - 2015

Joseph Bbosa served as a key figure in the UPC leadership structure, advocating for party reforms and strengthening grassroots organization. His dedication to the party's historical principles earned him respect across the political spectrum.
